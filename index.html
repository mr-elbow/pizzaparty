Phase 2: Structural Development

Ensure a Target Finished Dough Temperature of 75°F - 78°F. This range is the "sweet spot" where fermentation activity remains vigorous but the gluten doesn't degrade prematurely.

Phase 3: Bulk & Strengthening

Perform three sets of stretch-and-folds during the first 2 hours. This aligns the gluten network and traps the CO2 produced by the wild yeast.

Phase 4: Partitioning & Cold Ferment

Once bulk is complete, divide into your target weights. Tension the rounds into smooth, tight balls. Cold ferment in the refrigerator for 24–72 hours.

Phase 5: Tempering & Bake

Tempering the dough at room temperature is non-negotiable. Stretching cold dough causes "snap-back" and results in a denser crust.
