02 / Methodical Execution

Autolyse & Initial Hydration

Structural Development

Ensure your final dough hits a Target Finished Temperature of 75°F - 78°F. Use your ambient room temperature as a guide; if the air is cold, increase the water temperature slightly to maintain a healthy fermentation cycle.

Bulk Fermentation & Strengthening

During the first two hours, perform three sets of stretch-and-folds. Pull the dough upward until you feel tension and fold it over itself. Repeat this for all four sides. This builds the vertical strength required for a high-domed, airy crust.

Partitioning & Cold Ferment

Once the bulk period concludes, gently tip the dough onto a work surface. Shape into tight, smooth rounds by dragging the dough against the counter to create surface tension. Transfer to oiled containers and refrigerate for 24–72 hours.
