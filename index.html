Phase 2: Structural Development

Aim for a Finished Dough Temperature of 75°F - 78°F. This range ensures optimal yeast performance without risking gluten breakdown from overheating.

Phase 3: Bulk & Strengthening

Perform three sets of stretch-and-folds during the first 2 hours. This creates a vertical gluten structure and helps evenly distribute the thermal energy across the dough mass.

Phase 4: Partitioning & Cold Ferment

Once bulk fermentation is complete, divide the dough into your target rounds. Create tension by dragging the rounds against an un-floured surface. Store in the fridge for 24–72 hours.

Phase 5: Tempering & Bake

Tempering is crucial. Stretching a cold dough "shocks" the gluten, leading to snap-back and a rubbery cornicione.
